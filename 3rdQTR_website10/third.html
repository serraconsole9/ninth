I. Stream Overview

The members of Group 9 Serra Console launched their very first gaming livestream on Facebook last February 3, 2023, at 2:00 PM. The streamers of the group, Sean and Ellis, played Doors on Roblox, which is a first-person horror game with the goal of reaching the last door at Level 100, while avoiding entities that will attempt to kill the players within the lobby. The members aside the streamers on the other hand, naming Rhian, Raven and Aleah took part in technical and moderator duties.

The streamers took responsibility in following the stream outline, following every activity listed successfully. After engaging with the viewers and playing a round by themselves, Sean and Ellis invited the viewers to play the game with them. On the other hand, the moderators actively shared the stream on their personal accounts, and also interacted with the commenters in the stream’s chat box. Correspondingly, these actions helped in achieving the main goal of the stream: to have a relaxing and an out-going space for the stream’s viewers. However, during the stream, the streamers faced a few casualties in the technical side, mainly revolving around their audio and camera. Fortunately, the streamers addressed the issue at hand with ease and swiftly continued.

When the stream was about to end, the streamers asked their viewers to answer a form for their evaluation on Serra Console’s first livestream. Through this, the members gained knowledge in the things they did well, along with the areas they need to improve on.

II. Things Well Done

In this section the group will be discussing the results from the evaluation survey Serra Console conducted after their livestream. According to the viewers, the group of Serra Console excelled in terms of the following: the usefulness of the streaming information, the streamers’ knowledge about the content type, the streamers’ engagement and responsiveness, the design content, duration and platform venue of the stream, the accuracy of the stream outline, and in reaching the viewer’s expectation in the overall stream.

1. Use of Streaming Information

In the evaluation of the usefulness of streaming content, 75.0% of the viewers said that they can immediately use the streaming information shared during the live stream. This proves that Serra Console was successful in providing/sharing useful streaming information during their live stream.

2. Streamers' Knowledge

In the evaluation of streamers’ knowledge, 75.0% of the viewers said to have seen an excellent display of knowledge from the streamers during their first stream. While 20.0% of the viewers said that the streamers’ knowledge regarding the topic was good. This shows that streamers of Serra Console are well -experienced and knowledgeable enough about their streaming topic.

3. Streamers' Engagement

Throughout the live stream the streamers took their time to engage and entertain comments and requests from their viewers. This shows significantly in the results of their evaluation of streamers’ engagement, a total of 75.0% of viewers have said to have seen excellent engagement from the streamers, while 25.0% of the viewers said that the streamers’ engagement was good enough.

4. Streamers' Responsiveness

It is mentioned in Section 1: Stream Overview that Serra Console encountered a few technical problems during their first live stream, but the group was able to address the problem swiftly and was able to continue with their stream. This shows that the members of Serra Console are well-trained to be responsive to the problems they face along the way.

5. Design Contents

In the evaluation of design content, 80.0% of the viewers said that the group had excellent design content, while the remaining 20.0% said that the design content was good. This proves that the overall design content of Serra Console satisfied its audience.

6. Stream Duration

In the evaluation of stream length or duration, the group received 50.0% of responses stating that the stream duration was excellent, while the other 50.0% said that the stream duration was good. This shows that the viewers enjoyed the 1 hour and 17 minutes of stream time Serra Console decided on and later conducted.

7. Platform Venue

In the evaluation of the stream’s platform venue, a total of 75.0% responded that the platform venue used by Serra Console was an excellent choice, and 25.0% responded that the platform venue choice was a good streaming platform for the group’s content.

8. Accuracy of Stream

When asked about the accuracy of the streaming session outline, 50.0% of the viewers responded with Excellent while the rest either responded with Very Good or just Good.

9. Audience Satisfaction in Streaming Session

As seen in figures 11-12, almost all the responses range from excellent to good, with only 5.0% rating the Audience Satisfaction as Fair. proving that Serra Console’s first live streaming was successful in providing an enjoyable experience to the viewers.

III. Areas of Opportunity

1. Improve Streaming Layout

Serra Console received suggestions from the audience to “adjust the gameplay screen” and make it bigger as they found it hard to watch the streaming session because of its current size. The team also received suggestions to “improve their streaming chat box layering” because it was seen overlapping during the streaming session. After both of these feedbacks are taken into consideration, Serra Console members are now planning to improve their official streaming layout to fix and improve their gameplay screen and chat box layering to make it more pleasing to the audience’s eyes.

2. Better Audio Quality

A number of viewers also brought up the group’s poor audio quality during their first live stream, the viewers demanded louder and clearer audio from the streamers of Serra Console for there were times when they were hard to understand because of poor audio quality. The streamers of Serra Console assure their audience that in their next live stream, their audio quality will be better.

3. Wider Game Variety

Before the actual live stream starts, the streamers prepare a list of games in Roblox that they will be playing during the streaming session to avoid any time conflicts meaning that the games played during the streaming session are only limited to the list prepared beforehand. As a result, the audience suggested that Serra Console should have more variety of games to offer to make the streaming experience more enjoyable. Because of this, Serra Console members are now considering using a poll before every stream to let the viewers choose the live stream’s games in order to make fun and quality streaming contents that the audience will surely enjoy.

4. Introduction Incident

Before the introduction of the live stream, the group encountered an incident wherein the streamers of Serra Console forgot to mute their audios in the broadcasting application because of this the early viewers of the live stream heard the streamers having a conversation during the starting screen was shown. This incident somehow made the group seem unprofessional, which is why the group and the viewers see this as an area in need of improvement.

5. More Talks

In the Appendix A: figure 15 it is shown that 5.0% of Serra Console’s viewers suggested the group to have more talking sessions during their live streaming sessions. This is seen as an area of opportunity by the group because it is also shown at figures 5 and 13 that the viewers think the stream lacked in sharing relevant and new information. Which is why as resolution to these three feedbacks, the Serra Console streamers are thinking of preparing new segments wherein they share/talk about information that is both relevant and new to their viewers.

IV. Level of Engagement

The streamers promoted Serra Console and aired their very first livestream on their chosen platform Facebook. Before the actual livestream, the streamers posted promotional materials to encourage their target audience to like and follow their page, as well as to invite them to catch their first live scheduled on February 3, 2023.

According to the generated data and graphs from Facebook MetaBusiness Suite (See Appendix B, Figure 16 to 18) after an initial poster was posted and shared by the members in their personal accounts on January 28, the page reached 124 users, had 185 visits, and garnered 7 likes. Similarly, the members personally promoted Serra Console to their friends and relatives the day before the actual live, which is February 2, and immediately, the page saw an additional 182 reach, 409 new visits, and 13 new likes. Moreover, on the day of the actual livestream, the page had accumulated a total of 22 likes and 34 followers. Since the moderators also posted promotional material 1 hour before the livestream, the page once again saw an additional 182 in reach, 125 in visits, and 2 in likes.

Based on the limited overview found in Facebook’s Professional Dashboard, the stream accumulated a total of 333 views, 221 post reach, and 463 engagement. In addition, according to a comprehensive report generated by Facebook MetaSuite (See Appendix B, Figure 19 to 24), the livestream peaked at 27 viewers and had 34 reactions, 323 comments, and 9 shares. Similar to the data from the dashboard, the stream had 463 engagements and approximately 1,900 thousand minutes viewed.

Generally speaking, the level of engagement of the page before the actual stream was low, which in turn negatively impacted the overall performance of the stream. According to the Page Reach and Page Visits report, the increasing trend wasn’t consistent, indicating that the number of promotional materials posted weren’t sufficient to build a higher engagement. As a result, only few people were reached and informed of the date and time of the stream. Similarly, since the page only had 42 likes and 60 followers, only a small number of Facebook users were notified when the livestream started airing.

Furthermore, the level of engagement of the page affected the performance of the actual livestream in terms of reach. Like stated above, the engagement’s inconsistency led to the poor reach & overall engagement of the stream. The streamers think this could have been avoided if the promotional materials were posted daily and more consistently. Consistent posting of promotional materials could have also resulted in a higher number of likes and followers, thus, more people could have been notified once the stream was started. Due to this, the streamers are already planning a different approach to effectively build a higher level of engagement in the page, which would hopefully lead to our future stream’s better overall performance.

V. Viewership

Serra Console recently organized their first live stream on Facebook, which took place on February 3, 2023. This section will analyze the viewership gained by the team during their first live stream and provide insights on how they can improve for future streams. The analysis presented in this paper is based on the data collected from the generated statistics of the live stream on Facebook. Take note that throughout this section, "Appendix B: Facebook Stream and Page Data" will be referred to frequently, it can be navigated using the document outline.

1. Total Views and Shares

Upon thorough examination of the data, Serra Console has concluded that the first stream of the team gained low viewership.

According to Figure 19 (Refer to Appendix B), it shows that Serra Console received, at most, 27 views as the amount of peak live viewers throughout the duration of the whole stream. Furthermore, through investigating, the graph from Appendix B, "Shares Throughout The Live Stream" (Figure 22), shows that the total amount of shares from the live stream was only 9 shares. Taking that into account, Serra Console presumes the small amount of shares resulted in low viewership because it did not reach enough people. Although the team did share promotional posters and posts on their page before the actual day of the stream, inviting their friends and relatives alike to join and support them; The collected data suggests that they still lacked in terms of promoting materials and sharing for their stream, which they plan to improve upon in the future.

2. Viewer Demographics

Additionally, if we take a look at the demographics from Figure 25 and Figure 26 (Refer to Appendix B), it is seen that majority of the viewers reside in Metro Manila, with an 18-24 age range that most viewed the stream; Meanwhile the younger teens and older adults were less likely to watch. Serra Console's streaming time started from 2:00 PM - 3:15 PM on a Friday, which is usually simultaneous with the time when people are at school or work. This suggests that most people would be occupied during that time. The team has taken this into consideration and will discuss if they should adjust the streaming time for future streams.

3. Viewer Retention

On a positive note if we refer to the 24th figure (Refer to Appendix B), the constant flow of the line indicates that most, if not all viewers, stayed throughout the entire stream. This indicates that the viewers were entertained enough to have stayed throughout the whole stream, keeping a mostly constant number of views.

To summarize, Serra Console's first livestream on Facebook gained low viewership due to the lack of advertising of their stream. The selected streaming time also played a role in this, as it coincided with school and work time on a weekday. The team plans to improve their promoting materials and sharing for their future streams and consider adjusting the streaming time to increase viewership. The constant flow of viewers throughout the stream indicates that the viewers found the content entertaining, providing a positive outlook for future streams.
